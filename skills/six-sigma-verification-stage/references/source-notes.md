# Source Notes

This skill was created from a local review of `SSO-Book-Complete-LSS-BB.pdf`, identified in the PDF as the June 2018 edition from The Council for Six Sigma Certification.

The skill paraphrases and operationalizes concepts from the book rather than reproducing the book text. It does not bundle the PDF.

Key source concepts used:

- Six Sigma uses data, measurement, and statistical thinking to validate assumptions and reduce error.
- DMAIC phases: Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control.
- DMADV phases: Define, Measure, Analyze, Design, Verify.
- CTQ analysis translates customer or stakeholder needs into measurable requirements.
- Cost of quality and cost of poor quality help prioritize defects, failure costs, prevention, and appraisal work.
- Tollgates verify phase readiness before moving forward.
- Improve-stage verification can include solution selection, limited pilots, cost/benefit analysis, documentation, training, and signoff.
- Control-stage verification requires a control plan, monitoring, process-owner handoff, corrective action, capability/performance checks, and reflection for future improvements.
- Control plans should identify critical process steps, metrics, limits/specifications, units, measurement methods, sample sizes, frequencies, owners, record locations, and corrective actions.
- The expanded body-of-knowledge reference covers the book's major units: Six Sigma foundations, projects/processes, advanced DMAIC, beginner/intermediate statistics, Minitab/control workflows, experiments/DOE, brainstorming tools, process maps, and value stream maps.

Important pages reviewed:

- Pages 8-18: Six Sigma principles, data-driven decisions, variation, control, DMAIC/DMADV context.
- Pages 87-97: Quality, CTQ/CTC, CTQ trees, CoQ, CoPQ.
- Pages 120-186: DMAIC phases, improve verification, pilots, implementation planning, control plans, SPC/control concepts, control tollgate checklist.
- Pages 188-554: graphical analysis, probability distributions, correlation/regression, non-normal distributions, hypothesis testing, sample size, advanced control charts, Minitab workflows, ANOVA, DOE, brainstorming tools, process maps, and value stream mapping.

Use this source as method grounding, not as a substitute for project evidence. A plan still needs local requirements, codebase context, data, and user goals.
